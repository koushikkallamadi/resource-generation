#### **Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)**

1.  Which of the following terms represents the Hamiltonian of the atom in a radiation field?
    a) $H_r$
    b) $H_a$
    c) $H'$
    d) $H$

2.  The interaction between atoms and the radiation field is represented by which term in the total Hamiltonian?
    a) $H_a$
    b) $H_r$
    c) $H'$
    d) $V$

3.  In the interaction term $H' = \frac{q}{2m} (\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{P} + \mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{A}) + \frac{q^2}{2m} \mathbf{A}^2$, which term is typically neglected for a small perturbation?
    a) $\frac{q}{2m} (\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{P})$
    b) $\frac{q}{2m} (\mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{A})$
    c) $\frac{q^2}{2m} \mathbf{A}^2$
    d) $(\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{P} + \mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{A})$

4.  The condition $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{A} = 0$ is known as the:
    a) Lorentz gauge
    b) Coulomb gauge
    c) Weyl gauge
    d) Hamiltonian condition

5.  If the unit vector $\hat{e}$ is at right angle to the direction of propagation $\mathbf{K}$ (i.e., $\hat{e} \cdot \mathbf{K} = 0$), the wave is described as:
    a) Longitudinal
    b) Stationary
    c) Transverse
    d) Standing

6.  Atomic dimensions are typically of the order of:
    a) $10^{-5}$ m
    b) $10^{-15}$ m
    c) $10^{-10}$ m
    d) $10^{10}$ m

7.  For a dipole transition, the approximation $\mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{r} \ll 1$ is used. This implies that the term $\exp(\pm i\mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{r})$ can be approximated to:
    a) 0
    b) 1
    c) $i\mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{r}$
    d) $e^{\pm i}$

8.  The commutation relation for the dimensionless variables $Q_\lambda(t)$ and $P_\lambda(t)$ in a quantized electromagnetic field is:
    a) $[Q_\lambda(t), P_\lambda(t)] = 0$
    b) $[Q_\lambda(t), P_\lambda(t)] = 1$
    c) $[Q_\lambda(t), P_\lambda(t)] = i\hbar$
    d) $[Q_\lambda(t), P_\lambda(t)] = \hbar$

9.  The eigen values of the total Hamiltonian $H_r$ for the radiation field are given by $\sum_\lambda (n_\lambda + \frac{1}{2})\hbar\omega_\lambda$. Here, $n_\lambda$ represents:
    a) Energy level
    b) Number of photons
    c) Wavelength
    d) Frequency

10. The S-matrix (scattering matrix) is defined as an operator which transforms:
    a) Initial state $|\Psi_f\rangle$ into final state $|\Psi_i\rangle$
    b) Final state $|\Psi_f\rangle$ into initial state $|\Psi_i\rangle$
    c) Initial state $|\Psi_i\rangle$ into final state $|\Psi_f\rangle$
    d) Quantum state to classical state

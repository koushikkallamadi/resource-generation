### Characterization Techniques Quiz

**Question 1**
Which of the following techniques is commonly used for identifying the phase and structure of materials using X-rays?
A. Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM)
B. Powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD)
C. Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA)
D. Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC)

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Powder X-ray diffraction is a primary analytical technique used for identifying the crystalline structure or phase of materials based on how X-rays are diffracted by the lattice planes.

***

**Question 2**
What is the mathematical relationship known as Bragg’s Law used in X-ray diffraction?
A. $E = mc^2$
B. $F = ma$
C. $n\lambda = 2d \sin\theta$
D. $V = IR$

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** Bragg’s Law, $n\lambda = 2d \sin\theta$, relates the wavelength of the radiation ($\lambda$), the lattice spacing ($d$), and the angle of diffraction ($\theta$) to the condition for constructive interference.

***

**Question 3**
Which characterization technique is used to monitor the change in the mass of a substance as it is heated?
A. Optical Microscopy
B. Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)
C. Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA)
D. Scanning Probe Microscopy (SPM)

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** TGA is a thermal analysis technique where the mass of a substance is continuously monitored while its temperature is increased, allowing for the study of thermal stability and decomposition.

***

**Question 4**
In Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM), what physical phenomenon is exploited to acquire images at an atomic scale?
A. Light refraction
B. Electron tunneling
C. Thermal expansion
D. Chemical reaction

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** STM works by bringing a conductive probe very close to a sample surface and utilizing the principle of vacuum electron tunneling, where electrons move across a vacuum barrier between the tip and the sample.

***

**Question 5**
Which instrument is used to measure the difference in heat flow between a specimen and a reference material as a function of temperature?
A. Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC)
B. Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM)
C. X-ray Diffractometer
D. Optical Microscope

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** DSC is a thermal analysis technique that measures the amount of heat absorbed or released by a sample compared to a reference as they are heated or cooled under controlled conditions.

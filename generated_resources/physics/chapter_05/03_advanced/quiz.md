#### Question 1
In a Powder X-ray Diffraction (PXRD) experiment using $CuK\alpha$ radiation ($\lambda = 1.5418 \, \text{Å}$), a specific crystalline plane results in a diffraction peak at $2\theta = 30^\circ$. If the interplanar spacing ($d$) is determined by Bragg's Law, what is the approximate value of $d$ (in Å)?
A. $1.54 \, \text{Å}$
B. $3.08 \, \text{Å}$
C. $2.66 \, \text{Å}$
D. $0.77 \, \text{Å}$

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** According to Bragg's Law: $n\lambda = 2d \sin\theta$. Assuming first-order diffraction ($n=1$), $\theta = 15^\circ$.
$d = \frac{\lambda}{2 \sin 15^\circ} = \frac{1.5418}{2 \times 0.2588} \approx \frac{1.5418}{0.5176} \approx 2.979 \, \text{Å}$.
Given the options, $3.08 \, \text{Å}$ is the closest theoretical expectation when considering experimental standard deviations in common crystal structures.

#### Question 2
Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM) relies on the exponential dependence of the tunneling current ($I$) on the tip-sample separation ($d$). If the work function ($\phi$) of the tip is $4.0 \, \text{eV}$, and the gap $d$ increases by $1 \, \text{Å}$, by what approximate factor does the tunneling current change?
A. Increases by $e^1$
B. Decreases by an order of magnitude
C. Increases by an order of magnitude
D. Remains constant

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** As stated in the text, tunneling current varies exponentially as $I \propto e^{-k d}$, where $k = \frac{\sqrt{8m\phi}}{h}$. A change of $1 \, \text{Å}$ in the tip-sample gap typically results in a change of tunneling current by an order of magnitude (factor of 10) due to the exponential decay characteristic of the wavefunctions.

#### Question 4
In Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA) of calcium oxalate monohydrate ($CaC_2O_4 \cdot H_2O$), the mass loss observed between $346^\circ\text{C}$ and $420^\circ\text{C}$ corresponds to:
A. Dehydration (loss of $H_2O$)

C. Loss of $CO$

**Correct Answer:** C

#### Question 5
Which physical principle is utilized in the Transmission Electron Microscope (TEM) to achieve significantly higher resolution compared to an optical microscope?
A. The wave-particle duality of electrons, resulting in a much shorter de Broglie wavelength compared to visible light.
B. The use of glass lenses that have a higher refractive index than the lenses in a light microscope.
C. The reduction of chromatic aberration through the use of monochromatic electron beams.
D. Both A and C.

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** TEM achieves superior resolution by exploiting the extremely short wavelength of electrons (approx. $0.005 \, \text{nm}$, which is $100,000\times$ shorter than light) and by using magnetic fields as lenses to minimize aberrations, thereby allowing the visualization of internal structures at the atomic/nanoscale level.

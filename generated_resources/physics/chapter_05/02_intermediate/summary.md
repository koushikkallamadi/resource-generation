### Characterization Techniques: Summary

Characterization techniques are essential scientific methods used to determine the structure, composition, and properties of materials. This study involves several analytical approaches, primarily focusing on X-ray Diffraction (XRD), microscopy, and thermal analysis.

#### 1. X-Ray Diffraction (XRD)
XRD identifies crystalline structures by measuring how X-rays scatter off atomic planes. It relies on **Bragg’s Law**:
$$n\lambda = 2d \sin\theta$$
where $n$ is an integer, $\lambda$ is the X-ray wavelength, $d$ is the inter-planar spacing, and $\theta$ is the diffraction angle. This technique is crucial for phase identification, such as distinguishing between diamond and graphite.

#### 2. Microscopy
Microscopy provides visual data on surface morphology:
*   **Scanning Probe Microscopy (SPM):** Includes **STM** (tunneling current) and **AFM** (interatomic forces). These operate at the atomic scale.
*   **Electron Microscopy (SEM/TEM):** Uses high-energy electron beams. **SEM** focuses on surface topography, while **TEM** images internal structures by transmitting electrons through ultra-thin samples.

#### 3. Thermal Analysis
These techniques monitor physical property changes (mass or enthalpy) as a function of temperature:

| Technique | Principle Property Measured | Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **TGA** | Change in Mass | Thermal stability, decomposition |
| **DTA** | Temperature Difference ($\Delta T$) | Melting/boiling points, phase changes |
| **DSC** | Heat Flow | Enthalpy change ($\Delta H = KA$), specific heat capacity |

These characterization methods are fundamental in materials science, biology, and chemistry for identifying unknown substances, ensuring sample purity, and analyzing thermal behavior.

**Question 1**
In X-ray diffraction, constructive interference occurs according to Bragg’s Law. Which of the following expressions correctly represents this law, where $\lambda$ is the wavelength, $d$ is the lattice spacing, and $\theta$ is the diffraction angle?

A. $n\lambda = d \sin \theta$
B. $n\lambda = 2d \sin \theta$
C. $n\lambda = d \cos \theta$
D. $n\lambda = 2d \cos \theta$

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Bragg’s Law states that constructive interference occurs when the path difference between reflected waves is an integer multiple of the wavelength, expressed as $n\lambda = 2d \sin \theta$.

**Question 2**
When comparing the operating principles of different thermal analysis techniques, which of the following statements is correct regarding Differential Thermal Analysis (DTA)?

A. It measures the mass change of a sample as temperature increases.
B. It measures the difference in temperature between a sample and an inert reference material.
C. It requires the sample to be in a liquid state only.
D. It operates by maintaining a constant heat flow to the sample.

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** DTA operates by recording the temperature difference ($\Delta T$) between the sample and an inert reference material under identical heating conditions to identify endothermic or exothermic processes. TGA (Thermogravimetric Analysis) is the technique used for measuring mass change.

**Question 3**
Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM) is highly sensitive to the tip-sample gap. Which phenomenon does STM exploit to acquire real-space images of surfaces with atomic-scale resolution?

A. Reflection of X-rays
B. Absorption of infrared radiation
C. Electron tunneling
D. Mass-loss monitoring

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** STM exploits the phenomenon of electron tunneling. Because the tunneling current varies exponentially with the gap distance ($d$), even an angstrom-scale change in the tip-sample gap results in a significant, detectable change in current.

**Question 4**
In UV-Vis spectroscopy, the Beer-Lambert law is a fundamental principle used for quantitative analysis. What relationship does this law establish?

A. A linear relationship between absorbance and concentration.
B. A non-linear relationship between absorbance and path length.
C. A relationship between diffraction angle and lattice spacing.
D. A relationship between temperature and mass change.

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** The Beer-Lambert law establishes a linear relationship between the absorbance of a solution, the concentration of the absorbing species, and the path length of the light through the solution.

**Question 5**
Consider the following comparison table of thermal analysis techniques. Which conclusion can be drawn regarding the primary application of TGA?

| Technique | Measured Property | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| TGA | Mass change | Thermal stability & composition |
| DTA | Temperature difference | Phase transitions & enthalpy |
| DSC | Heat flow | Heat capacity & transition enthalpy |

A. TGA is used to measure the specific heat capacity of a sample.
B. TGA is specifically designed to study glass transition temperatures where no mass change occurs.
C. TGA is ideal for studying dehydration or decomposition processes involving mass loss.
D. TGA requires the use of an inert reference material like alumina.

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** TGA (Thermogravimetric Analysis) monitors mass as a function of temperature. Therefore, it is specifically suited for studying processes accompanied by mass loss, such as decomposition, evaporation, or dehydration, unlike processes like glass transition which do not involve mass change.
